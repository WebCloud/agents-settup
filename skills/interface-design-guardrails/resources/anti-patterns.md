# Anti-Patterns — What to Avoid

## Process Anti-Patterns (from Interface Craft)

1. **Shipping at level 1-3** because it's easy to stop pushing
2. **Optimizing a single initial instinct** without exploring structurally different alternatives
3. **Trying to solve everything simultaneously** — especially with AI tools
4. **Starting from a blank page** instead of platform defaults
5. **Adding more features/scope/flourish** instead of refining what exists
6. **Filling AI-gained time with pedestrian work** instead of uncommon care
7. **Generating "good enough"** when the bar for good enough is collapsing
8. **Showing variants of the same idea and calling it range** — it's depth, not range
9. **Working at inappropriate fidelity** for the decision at hand
10. **Saying "it feels nice" or "the design is clean"** without articulating WHY

## Visual Anti-Patterns (AI Slop)

These patterns together signal generic, template-generated design:

1. **Inter/Roboto/system font as the only typeface** — hallmark of AI-generated UIs
2. **Purple-to-blue gradient** as primary color motif
3. **Rounded corners everywhere** with no size variation
4. **Gradient buttons** with white text
5. **Evenly-spaced 3-column grid** of feature cards with icons — the most common template pattern
6. **Generic stock imagery** or bland illustrations
7. **"Get Started Free" + "Learn More"** as the only two CTAs
8. **White background, light gray cards, blue accent** — the trifecta of template design
9. **Hover-only interactions** — useless on mobile (60%+ of traffic)
10. **Decorative elements that serve no purpose** — gradients, blobs, abstract shapes

## Neuve-Specific Anti-Patterns (from 10+ iterations)

These have been explicitly rejected by the user:

- **Gradients** — NO gradients, ever. Every color is flat.
- **Full-bleed -500 color blocks** — too aggressive. Use -50/-100 tints instead.
- **Desktop browser mockups** — Neuve is iOS-only. No laptop frames.
- **CSS-art phone mockups** — looked cheap, not premium
- **Abstract geometric illustrations** — concentric shapes around logo rejected ("does not do it")
- **Colored borders on cards** — rejected
- **Pure typography with no color** — too minimal, needs the warm three-color system
- **Numbered sections (01/02/03)** — removed per feedback
- **Identical card layouts for all features** — needs visual rhythm (alternating layouts)
- **Small dots as visual indicators** — looked like bullet points, not design elements
- **Hover-only interactions** — useless on mobile, rejected repeatedly

## The Core Test

> "For every decision, you must be able to explain WHY. If the answer is 'it's common' or 'it's clean' or 'it works,' you have not chosen — you have defaulted."

Every element should pass this test. If you can't explain why it's there, remove it.
