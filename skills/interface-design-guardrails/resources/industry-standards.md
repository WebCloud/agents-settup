# Industry Standards — Default, Then Innovate

## The Invisible Bar

Popular apps (Instagram, Notion, TikTok, iOS, Linear, Figma, YouTube, Telegram) form collective expectations. This average becomes an invisible bar: the **industry standard**.

If your product doesn't meet this bar:

- People immediately discount it
- They assume it's poorly designed, probably poorly made
- They won't give you the time of day

This applies to: portfolio work, prototypes, MVPs, sales demos, landing pages.

---

## The Process

### Step 1: Start With Platform Defaults

**For iOS apps**: Human Interface Guidelines, system components, standardized APIs

**For web**: Start with what a default shadcn + Tailwind app would look like:

- System font stack or clean sans-serif (then upgrade to a distinctive pairing)
- Standard spacing scale (4/8px grid)
- shadcn component patterns (buttons, cards, inputs)
- Tailwind's built-in responsive breakpoints
- Light/dark mode via CSS variables

### Step 2: Meet the Bar

Ensure your implementation matches the quality level of apps people use daily. Check:

- Typography: readable, properly scaled, good line heights
- Spacing: consistent, generous, on a grid
- Color: intentional, accessible contrast, cohesive palette
- Motion: smooth, purposeful, respects reduced-motion
- Touch targets: minimum 44x44px on mobile

### Step 3: Innovate Beyond

Once defaults are solid, improve and innovate:

- Distinctive typography choices (beyond system fonts)
- Brand-specific color language
- Custom interactions that feel delightful
- Details that show uncommon care

## Exemplary Products

### Family (Fintech)

- Looks like an Apple-made app
- Took iOS defaults (tableviews, view transitions), showed respect for platform standards
- Went beyond and innovated on every aspect
- "The platonic ideal of what a fintech app on iOS should be"

### Raycast (Mac)

- Feels completely native
- Everything ranges from a little to a lot better than Spotlight
- Respected the platform, exceeded it

---

## Key Takeaway

> Don't start from scratch and end up far below expectations. Start with defaults, understand standards, then innovate.

The goal is not to be different. The goal is to be **better** — starting from a foundation people already understand and trust.
